\section{Purpose}
In this study ordinal pattern features are used to analyse simulated time series data generated from deterministic sine, ARMA, AR, MA, and logistic map models. 
%%% ACF The logistic map is also deterministic. It will read better as "two deterministic signals (sine and logistic map) and a stochastic model (ARMA, including two particular cases, namely AR and MA).
The purpose of this study is to explore additional points in the entropy–complexity $(H \times C)$ plane that were not identified in our previous experiments. 
%%% ACF "identified", or "observed"?
In the earlier experimental design, the resulting points were mostly concentrated near the right-hand corner of the HC plane 
%%% ACF We don't know what this "HC plane" is. Please follow the notation you have already defined.
(Figure~\ref{fig:HC_plane} shows it clearly), which made them difficult to use effectively for time series clustering. 
%%% ACF Why such concentration makes it difficult to use the points "for time series clustering"? Where does the difficulty lie?
Therefore, we broaden the experimental design in order to generate $H \times C$ points that are more widely distributed across the plane. 
This approach allows for improved visualization and a better representation of entropy and complexity values throughout the plane.

Once a sufficiently wide coverage of points in the $H \times C$ plane is obtained, these data will be used to further compute additional entropy and complexity related features. 
In particular, measures such as R\'enyi entropy, Tsallis entropy, and Fisher information will be calculated. 
These features will then be used to construct a multinomial logistic model in order to evaluate their ability to distinguish between different time series models. 
%%% ACF The multinomial model is a possibility among many
Model validation will be performed to identify the most informative features.

Finally, the validated features will be used for time series clustering. 
In the clustering stage, we aim to investigate how different model classes can be differentiated using ordinal pattern based features. 
Visual inspection of the HC plane will also be used to support the interpretation of the clustering results.

\section{Methodology}

In this experiment, we consider stochastic, deterministic, and chaotic functions. 
%%% ACF Are chaotic a third category, or they belong to one of the others?
We then couple these models to examine how the points are displayed in the $H \times C$ plane. 
Below, we describe the simulation setup for our experiment.

\textbf{Simulation and Feature Construction:}
%%% ACF Make this a subsection, and write a gentle introduction to what will be done. Speak about the values the time index takes. State that the sine and logistic functions will be defined in the following
%%% ACF This study will be part of your thesis. You may assume that the ordinal patterns have already been defined. In your thesis, you will have to define the MA, AR, and ARMA models
%%% ACF Do you need to mention the replications here? 
\begin{itemize}
	\item \textbf{Embedding:} A fixed embedding dimension ($D = 4$) and time delay ($\tau = 1$) are used.
	\item \textbf{Model structure:} $\mathrm{AR}(2), \, \mathrm{MA}(2), \, \mathrm{ARMA}(2,2),$ a sine function $(f(t))$, and the logistic map $(L_t)$ are considered.
	\item \textbf{Simulation:} For each model and parameter set, $R = 50$ time series of length $n = 1000$ are simulated.
	\item \textbf{Features:} For each realization, the following quantities are computed:
	\begin{itemize}
		\item \emph{Entropy:} Shannon entropy.
		\item \emph{Complexity:} Statistical complexity measures 
		%%% ACF Why "measures" in plural?
		corresponding to Shannon entropy.
	\end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

In addition to this setup, we will compute R\'enyi and Tsallis entropies and the Fisher information measure, as well as the associated statistical complexity measures for each entropy type. 
Moreover, variances will be computed for each model to assess the stability of the features. 
%%% ACF What is "stability" in this sentence?
Confidence intervals for entropy and complexity will also be computed for each model to further assess stability. 
Once we have examined the spread of the points in the $H \times C$ plane, we will proceed with model validation and time series clustering.

The parameters for these models are summarized in Table~\ref{tab:parameters}. 
The experimental setup includes the following components:

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Three $\mathrm{ARMA}$ type models:
	\begin{itemize}
		\item $X_{\text{ARMA}} = \mathrm{ARMA}(2,2)$
		\item $X_{\text{AR}}   = \mathrm{AR}(2)$
		\item $X_{\text{MA}}   = \mathrm{MA}(2)$
	\end{itemize}
	
	\begin{table}[H]
		\centering
		\caption{Parameter settings for the $\mathrm{ARMA}$ models used in the simulation study.}
		\label{tab:parameters}
		\begin{tabular}{lcc}
			\toprule
			\textbf{Model} & \textbf{AR coefficients} & \textbf{MA coefficients} \\ 
			\hline
			$\mathrm{ARMA}(2,2)$ & $(-0.8,\; 0.1)$ & $(-0.8,\; 0.1)$ \\ 
			\hline 
			$\mathrm{AR}(2)$     & $(0.1,\; 0.8)$ & -- \\ 
			\hline 
			$\mathrm{MA}(2)$     & --             & $(0.1,\; -0.8)$ \\ 
			\bottomrule
		\end{tabular}
	\end{table}
	
	\item A logistic map model defined as
	\[
	L_t = r L_{t-1}(1 - L_{t-1}),
	\]
	where \(r = 3.8\) is used in the experimental setup, with initial value \(L_0 = 0.5\).
	%%% ACF Did you discard initial values?
	
	\item A sine function defined as
	\[
	f(t) = \sin(2\pi f t),
	\]
\end{enumerate}
%%% ACF Do not leave a blank line in cases like this; please refer to the LaTeX documentation
where \(f\) and \(t\) denote the frequency and time, respectively. 
For the experimental setup, the frequency is set to \(f = 0.05\).

We then construct convex combinations of these components, mirroring the simulation design:
%%% ACF What do you mean by this "mirroring"? 
%%% ACF Do not leave a blank line in cases like this; please refer to the LaTeX documentation
\begin{enumerate}
	\setcounter{enumi}{3}
	\item Convex combinations of the $\mathrm{ARMA}(2,2)$ process with the logistic map and sine function:
	\[
	X^{\text{(ARMA+Logistic)}}_t(w) = w\, X_{\text{ARMA},t} + (1 - w)\, L_t,
	\]
	\[
	X^{\text{(ARMA+Sine)}}_t(w)     = w\, X_{\text{ARMA},t} + (1 - w)\, f(t),
	\]
	where \(w \in \{0.1, 0.2, \ldots, 0.9\}\).
	
	\item Convex combinations of the $\mathrm{AR}(2)$ process with the logistic map and sine function:
	\[
	X^{\text{(AR2+Logistic)}}_t(w) = w\, X_{\text{AR},t} + (1 - w)\, L_t,
	\]
	\[
	X^{\text{(AR2+Sine)}}_t(w)     = w\, X_{\text{AR},t} + (1 - w)\, f(t).
	\]
	
	\item Convex combinations of the $\mathrm{MA}(2)$ process with the logistic map and sine function:
	\[
	X^{\text{(MA2+Logistic)}}_t(w) = w\, X_{\text{MA},t} + (1 - w)\, L_t,
	\]
	\[
	X^{\text{(MA2+Sine)}}_t(w)     = w\, X_{\text{MA},t} + (1 - w)\, f(t),
	\]
	again with \(w \in \{0.1, 0.2, \ldots, 0.9\}\).
\end{enumerate}

For each model and each weight value of \(w\), we simulate time series of length \(n = 1000\) and compute the corresponding entropy and complexity measures.


The experimental setup uses an embedding dimension of \(D = 4\). 
All simulations are generated using the \texttt{Mersenne\_Twister} pseudorandom number generator.

Referring to the Figure~\ref{fig:ARMALogistic}, in the $H \times C$ plane, the points corresponding to the $\mathrm{ARMA}(2,2)$  and logistic model (convex combinations) mainly lie on the right-hand side of the plot, indicating relatively high entropy and moderate to low complexity. 
The Table~\ref{tab:arma-logistic} shows the convex combination model starts at $w = 0$, which corresponds to the pure logistic process with low entropy ($0.594$) and high complexity ($0.348$). 
As $w$ increases from $0$ to $0.3$, the complexity decreases further, while the entropy remains relatively high. 
For larger weights, an interesting pattern emerges: the mixtures with $w = 0.4$ and $w = 0.9$ exhibit entropy and complexity values closer to those of the pure $\mathrm{ARMA}(2,2)$ model ($w=1$), and their entropy even exceeds that of the pure $\mathrm{ARMA}(2,2)$ model. 
The points corresponding to intermediate weights $w = 0.5, 0.6, 0.7$, and $0.8$ show consecutively higher entropy and lower complexity. 
This behaviour illustrates how the choice of weight in the convex combination substantially affects the entropy-complexity characteristics of the ARMA + Logistic model.

\section{Conclusion}
We can summarize our experimental setup based on the simulation results as follows.

For any stochastic model $\{X_t^{(S)}\}$ and deterministic model $\{X_t^{(D)}\}$, 
we define a convex combination
\[
%%% ACF Avoid forcing spacing in math mode; let LaTeX make the choice
X_t(w) \;=\; w\,X_t^{(S)} + (1-w)\,X_t^{(D)}, 
\qquad 0 \le w \le 1,
\]
where weight values ($w$) control the relative contribution of the stochastic and deterministic components.


In our experiments, $X_t^{(S)} \in \{\mathrm{ARMA}(2,2), \mathrm{AR}(2), \mathrm{MA}(2)\}$ 
and $X_t^{(D)} \in \{\text{Logistic map} (L_t), \text{sine}\ (f(t))\}$.

For each pair $(X_t^{(S)}, X_t^{(D)})$, the mapping
\[
w \longmapsto \bigl(H(X(w)),\, C(X(w))\bigr)
\]
traces out a trajectory in the  $H \times C$ plane as the weight $(w)$ moves from $0$ (deterministic) to $1$ (stochastic). In several cases, we observe a qualitative change in this trajectory around $w \approx 0.4$ and $0.5$. The convex combinations with the logistic map and those with the sine function begin to separate clearly in the $H \times C$ plane. 

The behaviour at $w = 0.9$ is consistent with this interpretation, since $w$ is very close to $1$, the process is almost purely stochastic, and the corresponding entropy and complexity values lie near those of the underlying stochastic model. Small deviations at $w = 0.9$ can therefore be viewed as finite sample effects rather than a different regime.

In contrast, the MA(2)+Sine combinations yield comparatively low entropy and low complexity for certain weights, indicating that this particular pairing produces trajectories that remain close to the stochastic corner of the $H \times C$ plane. This motivates selecting a subset of models and weights whose trajectories jointly provide good coverage of the $H \times C$ plane, while remaining well separated from each other.


\section{Future work}
As shown in Figure~\ref{fig:HCScatter}, some entropy and complexity points overlap, making them difficult to distinguish. Therefore, we can exclude these overlapping combinations from our future experimental design. The final experimental setup will be based on this refined selection.
